chicken tikka masala

Sure! Here’s a simple recipe for chicken tikka masala: ### Ingredients #### For the Chicken Marinade: - 500g (1 lb) boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces - 1 cup plain yogurt - 2 tablespoons lemon juice - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil - 2 teaspoons garam masala - 1 teaspoon ground cumin - 1 teaspoon ground coriander - 1 teaspoon paprika (for color) - 1 teaspoon turmeric - 1 teaspoon salt - 1 teaspoon minced garlic - 1 teaspoon minced ginger - 1-2 green chilies (optional, for heat) #### For the Masala Sauce: - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee - 1 large onion, finely chopped - 2-3 cloves garlic, minced - 1 teaspoon minced ginger - 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes - 1 teaspoon ground cumin - 1 teaspoon ground coriander - 1 teaspoon garam masala - 1 teaspoon paprika - 1 cup heavy cream - Salt to taste - Fresh cilantro for garnish ### Instructions 1. **Marinate the Chicken:** - In a bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, oil, garam masala, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, salt, garlic, ginger, and green chilies. - Add chicken pieces and coat well. Cover and marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for best flavor). 2. **Cook the Chicken:** - Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Line a baking sheet with foil. - Spread the marinated chicken on the sheet and bake for about 15-20 minutes until cooked through. Alternatively, you can grill or pan-fry the chicken. 3. **Prepare the Sauce:** - In a large pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until golden brown. - Add garlic and ginger; cook for another minute until fragrant. - Stir in crushed tomatoes, cumin, coriander, garam masala, and paprika. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. - Add the cream and stir well. Simmer for another 5-10 minutes. Adjust salt to taste. 4. **Combine:** - Add the cooked chicken to the sauce and stir to coat. Let it simmer for a few minutes to blend the flavors. 5. **Serve:** - Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with rice or naan.

murgir jhol

Sure! Here’s a Bengali-style chicken curry recipe, known as **"Murgir Jhol"**: ### Ingredients - **For the Marinade:** - 500g (1 lb) chicken, cut into pieces - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder - 1 teaspoon red chili powder - Salt to taste - 1 tablespoon mustard oil (or any cooking oil) - **For the Curry:** - 2 tablespoons mustard oil - 1 large onion, finely sliced - 1 teaspoon ginger paste - 1 teaspoon garlic paste - 2 green chilies (slit) - 2 medium tomatoes, chopped - 1 teaspoon cumin powder - 1 teaspoon coriander powder - 1 teaspoon garam masala - 1-2 bay leaves - 2-3 whole cloves - 2-3 green cardamom pods - Salt to taste - Fresh cilantro for garnish ### Instructions 1. **Marinate the Chicken:** - In a bowl, mix chicken pieces with turmeric, red chili powder, salt, and mustard oil. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes. 2. **Cook the Chicken:** - Heat mustard oil in a large pan until hot. Add bay leaves, cloves, and cardamom. Sauté for a minute until fragrant. - Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown. - Stir in ginger and garlic paste, and cook for another minute. 3. **Add Tomatoes and Spices:** - Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until tomatoes are soft. - Stir in cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the oil separates from the masala. 4. **Combine with Chicken:** - Add the marinated chicken to the pan and mix well to coat with the spices. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. 5. **Simmer:** - Add about 1-1.5 cups of water (adjust based on how much gravy you want). Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. - Stir in garam masala towards the end. 6. **Serve:** - Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with steamed rice or parathas.

Kung Pao Chicken

Sure! Here’s a popular Chinese dish you might enjoy: **Kung Pao Chicken**. ### Ingredients - **For the Marinade:** - 500g (1 lb) boneless chicken breast, diced - 1 tablespoon soy sauce - 1 tablespoon rice wine or sherry - 1 teaspoon cornstarch - **For the Stir-Fry:** - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil - 2-3 dried red chilies (adjust for spice) - 1/2 cup peanuts (roasted or fried) - 1 bell pepper (diced) - 2 green onions (chopped) - **For the Sauce:** - 2 tablespoons soy sauce - 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce - 1 tablespoon rice vinegar - 1 tablespoon sugar - 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water ### Instructions 1. **Marinate the Chicken:** - In a bowl, combine diced chicken with soy sauce, rice wine, and cornstarch. Let it marinate for about 15-30 minutes. 2. **Prepare the Sauce:** - In another bowl, mix soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and the cornstarch slurry. Set aside. 3. **Stir-Fry:** - Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large pan over high heat. - Add dried red chilies and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant. - Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until it’s cooked through (about 5-7 minutes). - Add the bell pepper and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes. 4. **Combine with Sauce:** - Pour in the prepared sauce and stir well to coat the chicken and vegetables. Let it cook for another 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens. 5. **Add Peanuts and Green Onions:** - Stir in the peanuts and chopped green onions. Cook for an additional minute. 6. **Serve:** - Serve hot with steamed rice or noodles. Enjoy your Kung Pao Chicken! If you have any other preferences or want different recipes, just let me know!
